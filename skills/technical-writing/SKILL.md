# Technical writing

1. **Lead with the answer / takeaway**, then support it. Don't bury it under preamble.
2. **Know the reader:** match their level; define jargon the first time or avoid it.
3. **Structure for scanning:** short sections, descriptive headings, lists for steps,
   tables for comparisons.
4. **Be concrete:** real commands, real examples, real paths — not vague description.
5. **Cut ruthlessly:** remove filler, hedging, and repetition. Prefer short sentences.
6. **Show, then tell:** an example before the explanation often lands better.

End with what the reader should do next when relevant. Read it back once and delete a
quarter of the words.
